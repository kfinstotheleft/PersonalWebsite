About me

Boise Idaho > Dhahran Saudi Arabia > Ojai California > Nashville Tennessee > Austin Texas > Portland Oregon.

Born and raised in Idaho, I grew up with dreams of becoming a downhill ski racer. However, those dreams came to a quick halt, when my family up-and-moved to Dhahran, Saudi Arabia when I was ten.

In Saudi, I had the time of my life attending an international school and traveling to over thirty countries all over the world. When it came time for high school, I chose to leave home and attend boarding school in Ojai California due to the limited sports opportunities in Dhahran. It was at this time that running became a strong influence in my life, and ultimately springboarded me into Division I cross-country and track at Vanderbilt University in Nashville, Tennessee.

At Vanderbilt, I studied biomedical engineering and Spanish and was heavily involved in lab-based research. Upon graduating, I took a job as an R&D engineer at Smith & Nephew, a medical device company in Austin, Texas, and started training with a professional running team sponsored by Adidas.

After a few years, I experienced unprecedented success in running, qualifying to represent Team USA in an international cross-country race in Scottland. This opened up a new opportunity to move to Portland, Oregon and train with some of the country's best athletes.

Although it was difficult to leave a job I loved, I took the chance of a lifetime to chase running goals that I knew had an expiration date. So here I am in Portland, loving every minute of living and training in the beautiful Pacific Northwest! Recently, I have started taking computer programming classes so that I can work as an embedded systems engineer at a biotech company.
